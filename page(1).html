Emperor Tewodros II

A fearless unifier who fought valiantly to modernize and strengthen Ethiopia.

Here's a time line of Emperor Tewodros II's life:

  • 1818 - Born as Kassa Hailu in Qwara, Begemder Province, Ethiopian Empire.
  • 1835 - Begins his rise to power by leading a band of warriors and gaining control over various regions.
  • 1847 - Marries Tewabech Ali, daughter of Dejazmach Ali Faris, strengthening his political alliances.
  • 1853 - Defeats Ras Ali II at the Battle of Ayshal, solidifying his power in the region.
  • 1855 - Ascends to the throne as Emperor of Ethiopia, marking the beginning of his reign.
  • 1855 - Begins efforts to reunify the Ethiopian Empire and end the Era of the Princes (Zemene Mesafint).
  • 1856 - Establishes his capital at Debre Tabor and starts modernizing the army.
  • 1862 - Sends a letter to Queen Victoria of the United Kingdom, seeking an alliance, which goes unanswered.
  • 1863 - Faces increasing resistance from regional nobles and external pressures.
  • 1864 - Imprisons British missionaries and envoys, leading to diplomatic tensions with the United Kingdom.
  • 1867 - The British government launches the British Expedition to Abyssinia to rescue the imprisoned Europeans.
  • 1868 - Commits suicide at the Battle of Magdala to avoid capture by British forces, ending his reign.

"I know their game. First, the traders and the missionaries: then the ambassadors: then the cannon. It's better to go straight to the cannon."

-- Emperor Tewodros II
